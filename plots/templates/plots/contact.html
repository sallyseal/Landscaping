SUMMARY & FUTURE WORK

This project produces 3D epigenetic landscapes for any possible pair of genes in the given dataset by calculating the potential landscape, or the steady state probability distribution given a pair of genes and their associated expression values, using Gaussian kernel density estimation. In addition, the mutual information between all possible gene pairs is calculated and displayed in order to inform on which of these landscapes may be most interesting. Also, PCA is used to further investigate the given dataset and gene expression overlaid onto the PCA plot allowing a thorough and visually intuitive exploration into differential gene expression within the MEP cell population. This exploration strongly hints that MEP cells may already be transcriptionally primed for a single-lineage output of either erythrocyte or megakaryocycte phenotypes [3]. This can be seen when analysing the expression of megakaryocyte or erythrocyte associated genes, respectively. The sub-population to the left on the PCA plot displays increased expression levels of megakaryocyte-associated genes when compared to the sub-population on the right, while the sub-population to the right exhibits increased expression levels of erythrocyte-associated genes. This indicates that these cells may be transcriptionally primed to become megakaryocyte or erythrocyte cells, specifically and in fact do not have bipotent differential potential.

In addition to the analyses carried out, the data forms part of a fully fledged Django website comprising a SQL relational database allowing for extreme versatility and functionality. The website is scalable and is able to take any dataset containing single cell expression values and produce the same visually intuitive investigation.

SUMMARY & FUTURE WORK

Considering the timeline of the project and that a large portion of the time was dedicated to the website to ensure that the data analyses undertaken were visually intuitive and fully interactive, there is scope for further exploration. Since single cell data is often zero inflated it would be informative to assess other dimensionality reduction methods such as Zero Inflated Factor Analysis and its impact on the dataset [9]. In future it would also be interesting to assess competing methods for energy landscape construction or attempt to learn multiple single cell trajectories using Overlapping Mixtures of Gaussian Processes (OMGP) [10]. Lastly, it would also be informative to apply the approach of this study to time-course data so that the landscapes may be computed at different time points thereby creating a method to analyse the evolution of the landscape over time and provide a superficial overview of cell-fate development over time.
