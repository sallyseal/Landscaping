EXPLORING HETEROGENEITY IN THE DATASET

The principal component analysis plot below shows how MEP cells may be further divided into two sub-populations based on principal component 1 which accounts for just under 10% of the variance explained. Each dot on the plot represents a single MEP cell. In order to further explore these sub-populations based on their gene expression activity, the user may make use of the interactive plot below.

From the drop down menu, select the gene whose expression level you wish to assess in the single cells comprising the plot. Once selected, the expression intensity of the selected gene is overlaid onto the PCA plot with dark coloured cells representing low expression, green coloured cells medium expression and yellow coloured cells high expression (refer to the colour bar associated with the plot). This allows for a visually intuitive and interactive way to explore differential gene expression in this MEP cell dataset. The table to the right indicates which genes within this dataset are known to be associated with an erythrocyte or megakaryocyte phenotype specifically. By overlaying these particular genesâ€™ expression, it opens up an investigation into whether cells may be transcriptionally primed for a single lineage output (erythrocyte or megakaryocyte) at the MEP level already.
